Problem 1

Three of the vertices of a parallelogram, ABCD, are \(A(-5,-1)\), \(B(-1,-4)\) and \(C(6,-2)\).

(a) Find the midpoint of AC.

Midpoint Formula: The midpoint of a line segment with endpoints \( (x_1, y_1) \) and \( (x_2, y_2) \) is given by \( \left(\frac{x_1+x_2}{2}, \frac{y_1+y_2}{2}\right) \).

Apply Formula:

$$M_{AC} = \left(\frac{-5+6}{2}, \frac{-1+(-2)}{2}\right)$$ $$M_{AC} = \left(\frac{1}{2}, \frac{-3}{2}\right)$$ $$M_{AC} = (0.5, -1.5)$$

(b) Find the coordinates of D.

Parallelogram Property: The diagonals of a parallelogram bisect each other. This means the midpoint of \(AC\) is the same as the midpoint of \(BD\).

Let \(D=(x,y)\). We know \(M_{AC}=(0.5,-1.5)\) and \(B=(-1,-4)\).

Set up Midpoint Equation for BD:

$$0.5 = \frac{-1+x}{2}$$ $$-1.5 = \frac{-4+y}{2}$$

Solve for \(x\): \(1 = -1+x \Rightarrow x=2\)

Solve for \(y\): \(-3 = -4+y \Rightarrow y=1\)

Coordinates of \(D\): \(D(2,1)\)

Problem 2

The distance between two points \(P(-2,a)\) and \(Q(a-2,-7)\) is 17. Find the two possible values of \(a\).

Distance Formula: The distance \(d\) between two points \( (x_1, y_1) \) and \( (x_2, y_2) \) is given by \( d=\sqrt{(x_2-x_1)^2 + (y_2-y_1)^2} \).

Apply Formula:

$$17 = \sqrt{((a-2)-(-2))^2 + (-7-a)^2}$$ $$17 = \sqrt{(a-2+2)^2 + (-(7+a))^2}$$ $$17 = \sqrt{a^2 + (7+a)^2}$$

Square both sides:

$$17^2 = a^2 + (7+a)^2$$ $$289 = a^2 + 49 + 14a + a^2$$ $$289 = 2a^2 + 14a + 49$$

Rearrange into a quadratic equation:

$$2a^2 + 14a + 49 - 289 = 0$$ $$2a^2 + 14a - 240 = 0$$

Divide by 2:

$$a^2 + 7a - 120 = 0$$

Factor the quadratic equation: We need two numbers that multiply to -120 and add to 7. These are 15 and -8.

$$(a+15)(a-8) = 0$$

Solve for \(a\):

\(a+15=0 \Rightarrow a=-15\)

\(a-8=0 \Rightarrow a=8\)

Possible values of \(a\): \(a=-15\) or \(a=8\).

Problem 3

The point \((-2,-3)\) is the midpoint of the line segment joining \(P(-6,-5)\) and \(Q(a,b)\). Find the value of \(a\) and the value of \(b\).

Midpoint Formula: The midpoint \( (x_m, y_m) \) of a line segment with endpoints \( (x_1, y_1) \) and \( (x_2, y_2) \) is given by \( x_m=\frac{x_1+x_2}{2} \) and \( y_m=\frac{y_1+y_2}{2} \).

Given: Midpoint \( =(-2,-3) \), \(P=(-6,-5)\), \(Q=(a,b)\).

Set up equations for x and y coordinates:

$$-2 = \frac{-6+a}{2}$$ $$-3 = \frac{-5+b}{2}$$

Solve for \(a\): \(-4 = -6+a \Rightarrow a = -4+6 \Rightarrow a=2\)

Solve for \(b\): \(-6 = -5+b \Rightarrow b = -6+5 \Rightarrow b=-1\)

Values of \(a\) and \(b\): \(a=2\) and \(b=-1\).

Problem 7

Find the coordinates of the point which divides the join of \((-1,7)\) and \((4,-3)\) in the ratio 2:3.

Section Formula (Internal Division): If a point \((x,y)\) divides the line segment joining \( (x_1, y_1) \) and \( (x_2, y_2) \) in the ratio \(m:n\), then \( x=\frac{mx_2+nx_1}{m+n} \) and \( y=\frac{my_2+ny_1}{m+n} \).

Given: \( (x_1, y_1)=(-1,7) \), \( (x_2, y_2)=(4,-3) \), \(m=2\), \(n=3\).

Calculate \(x\):

$$x = \frac{2(4)+3(-1)}{2+3}$$ $$x = \frac{8-3}{5}$$ $$x = \frac{5}{5}$$ $$x = 1$$

Calculate \(y\):

$$y = \frac{2(-3)+3(7)}{2+3}$$ $$y = \frac{-6+21}{5}$$ $$y = \frac{15}{5}$$ $$y = 3$$

Coordinates of the point: \((1,3)\)

Problem 8

Find the coordinates of the point of division:

Since the ratio is 1:1, the point of division is the midpoint.

Midpoint Formula: \( x=\frac{x_1+x_2}{2}, y=\frac{y_1+y_2}{2} \).

Calculate \(x\):

$$x = \frac{1+(-4)}{2}$$ $$x = \frac{-3}{2}$$ $$x = -1.5$$

Coordinates of the point of division: \((-1.5,0)\)

Problem 10

Find the area of a rhombus if its vertices are \(A(3,0)\), \(B(4,5)\), \(C(-1,4)\) and \(D(-2,-1)\). [Hint: Area of rhombus \( = \frac{1}{2} \) product of its diagonals]

Calculate the length of diagonal AC:

Distance Formula: \(d=\sqrt{(x_2-x_1)^2 + (y_2-y_1)^2}\).

\(A(3,0)\) and \(C(-1,4)\).

$$AC = \sqrt{(-1-3)^2 + (4-0)^2}$$ $$AC = \sqrt{(-4)^2 + (4)^2}$$ $$AC = \sqrt{16+16}$$ $$AC = \sqrt{32}$$ $$AC = \sqrt{16 \times 2}$$ $$AC = 4\sqrt{2}$$

Calculate the length of diagonal BD:

\(B(4,5)\) and \(D(-2,-1)\).

$$BD = \sqrt{(-2-4)^2 + (-1-5)^2}$$ $$BD = \sqrt{(-6)^2 + (-6)^2}$$ $$BD = \sqrt{36+36}$$ $$BD = \sqrt{72}$$ $$BD = \sqrt{36 \times 2}$$ $$BD = 6\sqrt{2}$$

Calculate the area of the rhombus:

Area \( = \frac{1}{2} \times AC \times BD \)

$$\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times (4\sqrt{2}) \times (6\sqrt{2})$$ $$\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times 24 \times (\sqrt{2} \times \sqrt{2})$$ $$\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times 24 \times 2$$ $$\text{Area} = 24 \text{ square units.}$$

Problem 11

Find the ratio in which the line segment joining the points \((-3,10)\) and \((6,-8)\) is divided by \((-1,6)\).

Section Formula (Internal Division): Let the ratio be \(k:1\). \( x=\frac{kx_2+1x_1}{k+1} \) \( y=\frac{ky_2+1y_1}{k+1} \)

Given: \( (x_1, y_1)=(-3,10) \), \( (x_2, y_2)=(6,-8) \), point of division \( =(-1,6) \).

Use the x-coordinate to find \(k\):

$$-1 = \frac{k(6)+1(-3)}{k+1}$$ $$-1(k+1) = 6k-3$$ $$-k-1 = 6k-3$$ $$-1+3 = 6k+k$$ $$2 = 7k$$ $$k = \frac{2}{7}$$

Use the y-coordinate to verify \(k\) (optional, but good practice):

$$6 = \frac{k(-8)+1(10)}{k+1}$$ $$6(k+1) = -8k+10$$ $$6k+6 = -8k+10$$ $$6k+8k = 10-6$$ $$14k = 4$$ $$k = \frac{4}{14}$$ $$k = \frac{2}{7}$$

Ratio: The ratio is 2:7.

Problem 12

\(\triangle KLM\) has vertices \(K(5,18)\), \(L(10,14)\) and \(M(5,10)\).

(a) Find the length of each side.

Distance Formula: \(d=\sqrt{(x_2-x_1)^2 + (y_2-y_1)^2}\).

Length of KL: \(K(5,18)\) and \(L(10,14)\).

$$KL = \sqrt{(10-5)^2 + (14-18)^2}$$ $$KL = \sqrt{(5)^2 + (-4)^2}$$ $$KL = \sqrt{25+16}$$ $$KL = \sqrt{41}$$

Length of LM: \(L(10,14)\) and \(M(5,10)\).

$$LM = \sqrt{(5-10)^2 + (10-14)^2}$$ $$LM = \sqrt{(-5)^2 + (-4)^2}$$ $$LM = \sqrt{25+16}$$ $$LM = \sqrt{41}$$

Length of KM: \(K(5,18)\) and \(M(5,10)\).

$$KM = \sqrt{(5-5)^2 + (10-18)^2}$$ $$KM = \sqrt{(0)^2 + (-8)^2}$$ $$KM = \sqrt{0+64}$$ $$KM = \sqrt{64}$$ $$KM = 8$$

(b) Find the perimeter of \(\triangle KLM\).

Perimeter: Sum of the lengths of all sides.

$$\text{Perimeter} = KL+LM+KM$$ $$\text{Perimeter} = \sqrt{41} + \sqrt{41} + 8$$ $$\text{Perimeter} = 2\sqrt{41} + 8$$

(c) Find the area of \(\triangle KLM\).

Observation: Since K and M have the same x-coordinate, the side KM is a vertical line. This means KM can be considered the base, and the height will be the perpendicular distance from L to the line containing KM. The height is the absolute difference in the x-coordinates of L and K (or M).

Base: \(KM=8\).

Height: The perpendicular distance from \(L(10,14)\) to the line \(x=5\) (containing KM) is \(|10-5|=5\).

Area of a triangle: Area \( = \frac{1}{2} \times \text{base} \times \text{height} \).

$$\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times KM \times (\text{x-coordinate of L - x-coordinate of K})$$ $$\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times 8 \times 5$$ $$\text{Area} = 20 \text{ square units.}$$

Problem 17

Find the coordinates of the points of trisection of the line segment joining the point \((3,-3)\) and \((6,9)\).

Trisection: Trisection means dividing the line segment into three equal parts. There will be two points of trisection.

Point 1 (\(P_1\)) divides the segment in the ratio 1:2.

Point 2 (\(P_2\)) divides the segment in the ratio 2:1.

Section Formula (Internal Division): \( x=\frac{mx_2+nx_1}{m+n} \) and \( y=\frac{my_2+ny_1}{m+n} \).

Given: \( (x_1, y_1)=(3,-3) \), \( (x_2, y_2)=(6,9) \).

For Point 1 (\(P_1\)), ratio 1:2 (\(m=1,n=2\)):

$$x_{P_1} = \frac{1(6)+2(3)}{1+2} = \frac{6+6}{3} = \frac{12}{3} = 4$$ $$y_{P_1} = \frac{1(9)+2(-3)}{1+2} = \frac{9-6}{3} = \frac{3}{3} = 1$$ $$P_1=(4,1)$$

For Point 2 (\(P_2\)), ratio 2:1 (\(m=2,n=1\)):

$$x_{P_2} = \frac{2(6)+1(3)}{2+1} = \frac{12+3}{3} = \frac{15}{3} = 5$$ $$y_{P_2} = \frac{2(9)+1(-3)}{2+1} = \frac{18-3}{3} = \frac{15}{3} = 5$$ $$P_2=(5,5)$$

Coordinates of the points of trisection: \((4,1)\) and \((5,5)\).

Problem 20

The line \(y=x-3\) meets the curve \(y^2=4x\) at the points A and B.

(a) Find the coordinates of the midpoint of AB.

Find the points of intersection (A and B):

Substitute \(y=x-3\) into \(y^2=4x\):

$$(x-3)^2 = 4x$$ $$x^2 - 6x + 9 = 4x$$ $$x^2 - 10x + 9 = 0$$

Factor the quadratic equation:

$$(x-1)(x-9) = 0$$

Solve for \(x\): \(x=1\) or \(x=9\).

Find corresponding \(y\) values using \(y=x-3\):

If \(x=1\), \(y=1-3=-2\). So, point \(A=(1,-2)\).

If \(x=9\), \(y=9-3=6\). So, point \(B=(9,6)\).

Find the midpoint of AB:

Midpoint Formula: \(M_{AB} = \left(\frac{x_1+x_2}{2}, \frac{y_1+y_2}{2}\right)\).

Apply Formula:

$$M_{AB} = \left(\frac{1+9}{2}, \frac{-2+6}{2}\right)$$ $$M_{AB} = \left(\frac{10}{2}, \frac{4}{2}\right)$$ $$M_{AB} = (5,2)$$

(b) Find the length of the line segment AB.

Distance Formula: \(d=\sqrt{(x_2-x_1)^2 + (y_2-y_1)^2}\).

Given: \(A(1,-2)\) and \(B(9,6)\).

Calculate Length:

$$AB = \sqrt{(9-1)^2 + (6-(-2))^2}$$ $$AB = \sqrt{(8)^2 + (6+2)^2}$$ $$AB = \sqrt{(8)^2 + (8)^2}$$ $$AB = \sqrt{64+64}$$ $$AB = \sqrt{128}$$ $$AB = \sqrt{64 \times 2}$$ $$AB = 8\sqrt{2}$$
